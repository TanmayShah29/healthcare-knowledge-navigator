TYPE 2 DIABETES MELLITUS — MANAGEMENT OVERVIEW (SAMPLE GUIDELINE)

First-Line Therapy

Metformin is first-line therapy for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in most adult patients,
alongside lifestyle modification (diet and physical activity). Metformin treats Type 2
Diabetes Mellitus by reducing hepatic glucose production and improving insulin
sensitivity.

Contraindications

Metformin is contraindicated with severe renal impairment (eGFR below 30
mL/min/1.73m^2), due to an increased risk of lactic acidosis. Metformin is
contraindicated with acute or chronic metabolic acidosis.

Dosing

Sample recommended dosage for Metformin: starting dose 500mg once or twice daily with
meals, titrated gradually to a typical maintenance range, to reduce gastrointestinal
side effects. This is illustrative sample dosing only, not a real prescribing
reference.

Second-Line Therapy

If glycemic targets are not met on Metformin monotherapy, second-line options include
SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists. SGLT2 inhibitors treat Type 2 Diabetes
Mellitus and also reduce cardiovascular risk in patients with established
cardiovascular disease. GLP-1 receptor agonists treat Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus and are
associated with weight loss as a secondary benefit.

Monitoring

Clinicians should monitor for Hypoglycemia in patients on sulfonylureas or insulin.
Clinicians should monitor for Diabetic Retinopathy annually in all patients with Type 2
Diabetes Mellitus. Clinicians should monitor for Diabetic Nephropathy via annual
urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio testing.

Risk Factors

Obesity is a risk factor for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. Sedentary Lifestyle is a risk
factor for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. Family History of Diabetes is a risk factor for
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.

Symptoms

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus has symptom Polyuria. Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus has symptom
Polydipsia. Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus has symptom Fatigue.
