CARDIOVASCULAR RISK IN TYPE 2 DIABETES — SAMPLE RESEARCH SUMMARY

Background

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is a well-established risk factor for Myocardial Infarction
and Stroke. Patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus and established cardiovascular
disease benefit from glucose-lowering therapies with demonstrated cardiovascular
protection, rather than glucose-lowering alone.

Findings

SGLT2 inhibitors reduce the risk of Heart Failure Hospitalization in patients with
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, independent of their glucose-lowering effect. GLP-1 receptor
agonists reduce the risk of Myocardial Infarction and Stroke in patients with Type 2
Diabetes Mellitus and established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease.

Contraindications and Cautions

SGLT2 inhibitors are contraindicated with a history of recurrent Genital Mycotic
Infection without appropriate counseling and monitoring. SGLT2 inhibitors require
monitoring for Diabetic Ketoacidosis, including in patients with normal blood glucose
("euglycemic DKA").

Practice Implication

Treatment Protocol recommends assessing cardiovascular risk in every adult patient
newly diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, and preferring SGLT2 inhibitors or GLP-1
receptor agonists as second-line therapy when established cardiovascular disease or
heart failure is present.
